Gallaudet University REU AICT Research Intern

  The Gallaudet University Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) Accessible Information and Communications Technology (AICT) Program connected students from across the nation to study emerging technologies to help improve online accessibility for disabled, Deaf, or Hard of Hearing individuals. Amongst many different projects (which are shown in the video below), this program spearheaded research in AI interpretation, signing anonymity, and user experience with various platforms designed for the Deaf community. During this summer research experience, my team focused on sign language databases and their perception within the deaf community. After conducting research on existing platforms' front-end design, we conducted interviews with Deaf and Hard of Hearing community members. The overal goal of the study was to examine user's relationship with the platforms, and how to increase their engagement as a more social (or educational) platform. In preparation, the research group created prototypes using the design platform Figma. After recieving responses and feedback from our participants, we modified our design to accomadate for a more user-friednly and tailored design. While there was not sufficient time to construct a fully operational platform within our remaining time frame, our final presentation provided recommendations and statistics for future designers interested in modifying their platforms.
